Artist statement 2013: portal paintings

 
 
 

My current paintings contrast order and materiality within the same picture plane. The paintings explore the phenomenological sublime, contrasting natural phenomena or natural disasters with man-made interventions creating portals which are mixed with beauty and terror within the same frame. The painting process evolves through material coagulations of oil paint, which is poured and blown onto the surface. These random pourings are juxtaposed next to more controlled painted layers – this juxtaposition of order and materiality refer to elemental collision of disparate constructed worlds. In the painting Microscopic Magnitude I frame the image within a tondo format previously used in renaissance or baroque painting and architecture, however in a contemporary context I refer to the lens and the space of viewing through the lens from the position of the observer. The black grounds functions as dark vignettes creating a selective view- point through the lens or telescope. In paintings such as Worlds, Night Vision and Coriolis, the black grounds refer to the notion of black infinity and darkness, which Edmund Burke describes in On the Sublime and the Beautiful as the “infinite sublime”, evoking an unknowable terror or a sense of uncertainty of where we stand in space and what is before us.

In the recent series Portal Paintings there are a number of references and influences such as historical sublime landscape painting, contemporary artists such as Jack Goldstein, and virtual images of natural and man-made disasters or phenomena, gleaned from the media or internet. The proliferation of photographic images within the digital domain creates a mediated experience, a simulacra of events, which the viewer tries to unravel and filter in search for authenticity and meaning. I focus on details of images through framing and compositional structures, creating a selective view-point from a bigger picture.

This selection process focusing in on details presents an ambiguity within the paintings, one which is concerned with our association to images and implied narrative, yet on closer inspection the viewer is presented with formal layers of paint exploring space through material coagulations of paint in the form of order and disorder.
